How Grease Works In Pins And Bushings
Grease flow diagram showing pin, bushing, and purge path.
Grease enters through the fitting, fills the bushing clearance around the pin, acts as a lubricating buffer, and then exits as old grease is pushed out of the joint.
1. Grease Enters

New grease is pumped through the fitting and into the pin and bushing clearance area.

2. Film Forms

The grease creates a separating film so the pin rides on lubricant instead of direct metal-to-metal contact.

3. Contaminants Purge

As fresh grease fills the joint, old grease, water, and dirt are forced toward the edges and out of the joint.

Maintenance Do / Don't
Do
  • Use Hitachi Construction Machinery Genuine Greases whenever possible for optimum machine performance.
  • If using alternative grease, match NLGI 2 EP specification.
  • If not using genuine Hitachi grease, use grease conforming to EP-2.
  • Clean grease fittings before applying grease.
  • Store grease in sealed containers away from dirt and moisture.
Don't
  • Do not use grease that does not meet the required EP-2 specification.
  • Do not use unsuitable grease, because the machine may suffer damage.
  • Do not apply contaminated grease from damaged cartridges.
  • Do not mix unknown thickener types without compatibility checks.
Important Warning

Do not use grease that does not meet the required specification or requirements.

  • Hitachi recommends Genuine Greases for optimum machine performance.
  • If not using genuine Hitachi grease, use grease conforming to EP-2.
  • Unsuitable grease may cause machine damage.
